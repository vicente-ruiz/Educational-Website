Overview

Educational Website

This educational website is designed to raise awareness about common cybersecurity threats through interactive modules. It covers keylogging, reverse shell attacks, and credential manipulation, providing clear explanations, diagrams, videos, and quizzes to help users understand how these attacks work and how to stay protected.

Keylogger

Introduction

Keyloggers, such as the Pico Ducky, are tools designed to record keystrokes on a target device. While they have legitimate uses, such as monitoring employee productivity or parental control, they are often associated with malicious activities like data theft.[1] This research aims to provide an in-depth understanding of how a Pico Ducky operates, its logging methods, evasion techniques, and the ethical implications of its use.

Reverse Shell Attacks

Introduction

Reverse shell attacks allow an attacker to establish remote access to a compromised system. This method is widely used in post-exploitation stages and red team assessments.

Basic Reverse Shell Operation

An attacker initiates a connection to a listening server, gaining full remote control over the compromised machine.

Credential Manipulation

Introduction

Credential manipulation involves modifying or stealing authentication credentials to gain unauthorized access to a system.

Techniques
